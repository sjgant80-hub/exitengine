How to find your first client

Almost always: they are already in your phone. Your contacts list is the answer. The question is just which 10 names to start with.

The maths of warm vs cold

A cold message on LinkedIn to a stranger has a reply rate of roughly 3-5%. A warm message to someone who knows you (worked together, met at a conference, were in the same university year) has a reply rate of 40-70%.

You don't need 100 warm contacts. You need 10. To find one paying client in 30 days, you need to message all 10, and you need to do it well.

The 10 names · how to pick them

Open your contacts. Open your LinkedIn. Open your email. Look for:

  1. Former colleagues who moved to other companies. They know your work. They have budgets. They have problems. Top of the list.
  2. Former bosses you parted on good terms with. Especially if they moved to another company or set up their own. They hire fast and they hire people they know.
  3. Former clients (if you were customer-facing). Direct value · they already paid for your work.
  4. Suppliers and vendors from your old role. They know the buyer-side of your industry. They make introductions.
  5. People who reached out to you in the last 12 months. Anyone who sent "hey would love to chat" while you were busy. Now you're not busy.
  6. People you mentored. They feel they owe you. They're now at more senior places. Great vector.
  7. One person you admire from a distance. The reach-out is just to acknowledge them. No ask. No pitch. Often opens an unexpected door.

Aim for a mix: 4-5 obvious closes (former colleagues), 2-3 warm long-shots (former bosses, mentees), 1-2 wildcards (someone you've never spoken to but should have).

The reach-out · the script that works

The pattern · 4 paragraphs · 80 seconds to read

  1. Acknowledge them (specific, recent, real)
  2. Quick update on your situation (factual, not woe-is-me)
  3. The hypothesis (you think you might be able to help with X — specific)
  4. The low-friction ask (15-20 minute call, two specific time slots)

Template (copy and adapt)

Hi {{ name }}, Hope you're well. {{ specific thing they did or said recently · LinkedIn post, new role, company news }} — saw {{ that thing }} and meant to reach out properly. Quick update from my side: I left {{ old company }} a few weeks ago and I'm now consulting independently, focused on {{ the specific thing you do }}. The reason I'm writing: I've been thinking that {{ their company or function }} might be at the stage where {{ specific problem you solve }} would be worth a conversation. No pitch — just 20 minutes to compare notes. If I can be useful, great. If not, it's been good catching up either way. Tuesday afternoon or Thursday morning work for you? {{ Your name }}

Why this works

  • Specific acknowledgement — proves you actually thought about them, not bulk-DM-ing your network
  • Factual update — no defensive over-explanation, no negative spin on the layoff
  • Specific hypothesis — shows you've done 5 minutes of research on their current situation
  • Low friction ask — 20 minutes is short, two specific slots is easy to accept
  • Symmetric tone — "compare notes" frames you as a peer, not as a job-seeker

Send them in batches of 3 · not 10 in one day

Why: each response teaches you something. The first 3 you send, you'll see what gets a reply and what doesn't. Adjust the script. Send the next 3 better. Adjust again. Send the last 4.

Cadence: 3 on Monday, 3 on Wednesday, 4 on Friday. By the following Monday you'll have responses from 4-7 of them. By month-end you'll have 1-3 conversations that turn into something.

What to do with the replies

If they say yes · the discovery call

20 minutes. Three questions: (1) What are you working on? (2) What's the most annoying thing about it right now? (3) If you could wave a wand and fix one thing, what would it be?

Listen for 80% of the call. Take notes. Don't pitch. If they ask "what would you do?", give one specific suggestion. Then offer to send a short follow-up email summarising what you heard and one concrete next step.

If they say "not right now"

Reply: "Totally fair · who else at {{ company }} or in your network would benefit from a similar conversation?" Referrals from warm contacts have similar reply rates to warm contacts. Each call should generate at least one referral.

If they ghost

One follow-up at day 7. "Just bumping this in case it landed at a bad time — no offence taken if not. Otherwise let me know if Wednesday or Friday next week works." One follow-up only. Then move on.

Open the CRM. Put the 10 names in.

That's the action. Don't read another lesson before you've done this. The names are already in your phone — copy them across, stage them, set yourself a reminder to send the first three Monday morning.
