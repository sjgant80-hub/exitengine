From 1 client to 10

Most independents stall at 1-3 clients. They earn enough to pay rent but never enough to build savings. The maths of getting from 1 to 10 — and the maths of WHY 10 is the right number, not 100.

The 10-client ceiling

You don't need 100 clients. Most independent consultants hit their happy stable state with 6-10 active clients at any one time. Why:

  • One client is fragile · they leave, you have nothing
  • 2-3 clients · the standard "freelancer" state · cash-flow tight, no leverage
  • 4-6 clients · you can choose which to retain · you can fire bad ones
  • 6-10 clients · you can charge premium because you have a queue · you can afford to take 6 weeks off · you have a real business
  • 10+ clients · you've outgrown solo work · you either subcontract, hire, or specialise harder

So the goal of "growing" is not infinite. The goal is to reach 6-10 and stay there with the right clients.

The pipeline you need to maintain 6-10 active clients

The 3-to-1 ratio

For every active client you have, you need 3 leads in the pipeline at various stages. Why: typical close rate from cold lead to active client is ~30% over 3-6 months. To replace 1 lost client takes 3 active conversations.

So for 10 clients you need ~30 active leads. That means you should always have:

  • ~10 leads in "Meeting set" or later
  • ~10 leads in "Contacted, awaiting reply"
  • ~10 leads in "Identified, not yet contacted"

If your pipeline drops below 30, schedule outreach. Don't wait until you've lost a client.

Three growth engines · in order of efficiency

1 · Referrals from existing clients · highest ROI

Every happy client should refer you to 1-2 others within 6 months. They will not do this spontaneously. You have to ask.

The script: "Thanks for the [project / month / quarter] — I'm glad we [specific outcome]. As I'm building the practice, the question I'd ask you: who else in [their network / industry] do you think could benefit from this kind of work?"

Direct, professional, not desperate. Half the time they'll think of 2-3 names on the spot. Other half they'll come back to you within a week.

2 · LinkedIn presence · medium ROI

Not influencer-style daily posting. Once a week, specific to your discipline, sharing a real observation from your work. Goal: when someone Googles "best [your industry] consultant in [your region]", your LinkedIn profile is on page 1.

The pattern that works: one post a week, each one telling a small story from a real engagement (anonymised). End with a question. Comment on 5 other people's posts in your network. Total time: 30 minutes a week. Result over 6 months: 2-4 inbound leads, slowly compounding network effect.

3 · Cold outreach · lowest ROI but always available

5-10% reply rate at best. Use when the pipeline is dry and the warm network is exhausted. Pick a specific role at a specific kind of company, write a specific message about a specific problem, send batches of 10-20 per week.

Never bulk-mail. Never use "I see you work in X..." openers. Personalise the first sentence to something verifiably about them. The Tutor can help draft these — paste in target details, get a draft, adapt.

Repeat business · the underrated engine

Most freelancers lose money by not asking

The most efficient revenue is the next contract from a client who already paid you. They know your work. They have the budget. They have related problems.

At the end of every engagement, ALWAYS ask: "This worked because [reason]. The next thing in this space that I'd recommend looking at is [related problem]. Would it be worth scoping that as a follow-on?"

One in three clients will say yes on the spot. Another one in three will return within 12 months. That means roughly two-thirds of your active clients should become repeat-business sources.

The pipeline discipline · weekly

One day a week — Friday morning works for most people — spend 90 minutes on the pipeline:

  1. Review CRM. Anyone in "Meeting set" who hasn't been followed up? Anyone in "Contacted" who needs a 2nd touch?
  2. Identify 3-5 new leads. From a target-company list, from a LinkedIn search, from a referral list.
  3. Send 3-5 outreaches (cold or warm).
  4. Write your weekly LinkedIn post (or two for the next week — bank them).
  5. Run the Analytics dashboard. What's the pipeline value? What's the close rate? What's the overdue invoice total?

Total time: 90 minutes a week. Result over 6 months: a self-sustaining business with 6-10 active clients and a queue.

Knowing when to stop

The signs you've made it

  • You can afford to turn down a client because they're a bad fit
  • You can take 4 weeks off without panic
  • You have 3+ months of expenses in a tax-and-emergency reserve
  • Your retainer income covers your fixed costs · project income is your upside
  • You can name 3 problems you've solved that you'd be remembered for

That's the state. It's not glamorous. It's stable, autonomous, and yours.
